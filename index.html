Tribute to Akira Toriyama

Manga Artist, Creator of Dragon Ball

5-4-1955 To 1-3-2024

About Akira Toriyama

Akira Toriyama, born on April 5, 1955, is a renowned Japanese manga artist and character designer. He is most famous for his manga series "Dragon Ball," which has influenced countless artists and spawned a massive media franchise.

Key Works

  • Dragon Ball (1984-1995)
  • Dr. Slump (1980-1984)
  • Crows (1993)
  • Tomato, the Girl from the Country (1990)
  • Dragon Ball Super (2015-Present)

Achievements

Throughout his career, Toriyama has received numerous awards and accolades:

  • Shogakukan Manga Award for "Dr. Slump" (1981)
  • Harvey Award for Best Manga (1991)
  • Tezuka Osamu Cultural Prize (1994)
  • Japanese Cartoonists Association Award (2002)
  • Industry Icon Award (2019)

Influences

Toriyama's work has been influenced by various artists and genres, including:

  • Osamu Tezuka - Pioneer of manga, known for "Astro Boy."
  • Western comics, particularly superhero comics.
  • Martial arts films and anime.

Personal Life

Toriyama is known for being quite private. He enjoys a simple life with his family and is an avid fan of video games and car design. His passion for these hobbies often influences his work.

Impact on the Industry

Akira Toriyama has had a profound impact on the manga and anime industries, setting trends and standards for storytelling and character development. "Dragon Ball" has become a cultural phenomenon, inspiring generations of creators and fans around the world.

Timeline of Major Events

  • 1978: Began working as a manga artist.
  • 1980: Published "Dr. Slump," which became a massive hit.
  • 1984: Launched "Dragon Ball," revolutionizing the shonen genre.
  • 1996: The first "Dragon Ball" anime series concludes.
  • 2002: "Dragon Ball Z: Battle of Gods" revitalizes interest in the franchise.
  • 2015: "Dragon Ball Super" begins serialization.
